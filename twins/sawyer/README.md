# The Sawyer demo twin

This is `work-twin`'s flagship demo: a digital twin of **Sawyer Middeleer** —
founder of Revi Systems, an operator-consultant who builds AI operating systems,
writes the *Exponential Operator* newsletter, and teaches AI operations. It ships
so you can see a real, runnable twin before you build your own.

The dossier is at [`../../agents/sawyer.md`](../../agents/sawyer.md). Worked
examples — one per use case — live in [`examples/`](examples/).

## Built only from public evidence

This is a **public demo in a public repo**, so it is assembled strictly from
Sawyer's public-facing writing:

- the *Exponential Operator* newsletter (published issues),
- his LinkedIn posts,
- a public guest post,
- Revi Systems' own published positioning and voice,
- Sawyer's published `writing-like-sawyer` voice method,
- and his public career history (Thrasio, Revel, Aomni, Duet).

No private correspondence, client work, meeting transcripts, financials, pricing,
or self-assessment instruments went into it. It names **no clients and no
engagement specifics** — where his source material references a specific
customer, the twin generalizes.

This is the plugin's own honesty standard, applied to itself: *build twins only
from evidence you have consent to model.*

## What it can do

The twin runs the five use cases in the twin's own voice and lens:

- **Pressure-test** a plan, positioning line, or artifact — surfaces the
  objections Sawyer would actually raise (where's the baseline? who owns this
  after handoff? did you pick the tool before the outcome?).
- **Thought-partner** a problem in his frame — recategorize what kind of problem
  it is, decompose the fuzzy worry, reduce to the one decisive question.
- **Coach** an operator on their own patterns, warmly and candidly.
- **Board seat** — hold one distinct seat among several minds over a decision.
- **Role-play** — react as Sawyer would in a live discovery call, pitch, or
  review.

It is strongest exactly where a public corpus is strong: **Voice, Lens, and
Signature.** The cadence, the mental models (AI is an org-design problem, not a
tool problem; work backward from the outcome; the AI-adoption ladder; deployment
over capability; measurement as the silent failure), and the disagreement moves
(recategorize, deflate the jargon, pushback-as-a-question, the confession-hook)
are all directly quotable from his published writing.

## What it can't do — thin by design

A public corpus can't see a person's interior, so two of the five dimensions are
**deliberately thin**:

- **Drive** — what actually moves him, his private goals and priorities. The twin
  models only what's *visible* in public writing (anti-hype, anti-gatekeeping,
  build-real-things). His real motives and goal stack are not in here.
- **Interior Shadow** — his private contradictions, fears, and blind spots. The
  twin carries only public self-admissions (over-delivery, people-pleasing, the
  capacity-drop on unloved work, marketing as non-native). The deeper interior
  isn't sourceable and isn't invented.

When a decision hinges on his private reasoning, the honest twin says so and
defers to the real Sawyer. It is a **simulation, not the person** — it commits to
nothing, and any stance older than a quarter is a hypothesis, not a fact. It is
also **falsifiable**: the [calibration log](../../agents/sawyer.md#calibration-log)
in the dossier scores its predictions against what the real Sawyer does.
