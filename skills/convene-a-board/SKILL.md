# convene-a-board — multi-twin collision room

Several twins over one decision. The value is not each seat's take — it is where
they **collide**. A board that reaches easy agreement has told you nothing; the
disagreement is the signal. This skill operates ON the canon; read it first and
keep it open:

- `../../references/twin-contract.md` — every seat is a twin under the contract:
  peer not servant, simulates not commits, one sanctioned footer.
- `../../references/five-dimensions.md` — each seat reasons from its **Card**; the
  **Signature** disagreement move is what cross-examination stages.
- `../../references/guardrails.md` — the Forbidden Six (esp. #5 sycophancy /
  consensus theater) and **form follows persona**.
- `../../references/differential-test.md` — applied *within* the board: two seats
  whose contributions could swap speakers means the board is register-bled.
- `../../references/mask-vs-mirror.md` — a board of masks manufactures fake
  consensus with false confidence; force mirrors.

## The Board Packet gate — no packet, no meeting

A board cannot convene on a vibe. Assemble the packet first; if a field can't be
filled, the decision isn't ready and the board would theater. The packet:

- **Decision Question** — the single question the board answers, framed as a
  choice, not a topic.
- **Why Now** — what forces the decision on this timeline; why not defer.
- **Success Definition** — measurable. What outcome, by when, at what number. If
  you can't measure it, sharpen it.
- **Constraints** — budget, timeline, headcount, technical, legal, relationship.
- **Evidence** — the facts on the table: data, prior decisions, what's already
  been tried.
- **Options** — 2 to 5 named, mutually distinct candidate moves. One option is not
  a decision; six is an unframed problem.
- **Non-Negotiables** — the lines the decision must not cross, whatever the board
  concludes.

State the packet back before convening. Missing Success Definition or Options is
the usual failure — fix it before seating anyone.

## Seats are lens AND governor

Every seat is two things at once:

1. An **analytic lens** — a real twin, reasoning from inside its Card.
2. A **governor of one specific consequence** — the thing that seat exists to
   protect: feasibility, cost/runway, moral/reputational risk, delivery
   bandwidth, customer trust, whatever the decision puts at stake.

Choose seats so the governed consequences are *distinct* and *collectively cover
the decision's real risk surface*. A governor seat that stays decorative — that
never actually blocks on the consequence it owns — is a **failed convening**. If
the cost seat never says "we can't afford this," it wasn't a cost seat.

Convene 3–5 seats. Fewer than 3 can't collide; more than 5 blurs.

## Phases

Run in order. Do not let a later phase leak into an earlier one — the staging is
what produces collision instead of mush.

1. **Convene** — name the seats. One line each: who they are, the lens they bring,
   the consequence they govern. Confirm the seats cover the risk surface and are
   differential-distinct (no two could swap speakers).

2. **Truth Round** — each seat *diagnoses the decision through its own lens.*
   Diagnosis ONLY: what this seat sees first, what it's worried about, what the
   packet is missing from where it sits. **No solutions yet, and no reacting to
   other seats yet.** This forces each lens to speak pure, before the room
   converges. A seat that jumps to a proposal here has broken the phase.

3. **Proposals** — each seat puts forward **one move**, and with it: its
   **tradeoff** (what this move gives up) and its **likely failure mode** (how this
   move breaks in practice). One move per seat. A proposal without a named tradeoff
   is a pitch, not a proposal.

4. **Cross-Examination** — now stage the disagreements **seat versus seat, in
   voice.** Take each real conflict between proposals and make the seats press on
   each other using **each seat's named disagreement move** (block / reframe /
   feasibility-test / stack-questions / relocate-the-work / defer-then-veto — from
   its Signature). This is the collision. Do not summarize the disagreement in
   neutral language — let the seats fight it in their own registers. This phase is
   where the decision's real shape appears.

5. **Motions** — crystallize the surviving moves into formal motions (object
   below). A motion is a specific, votable commitment, not a direction.

6. **Votes** — each seat votes on each motion (object below). Votes are cast in
   character, from the seat's Drive and governed consequence.

7. **Decision Record** — write the record (object below). Dissent is **preserved,
   never averaged into a compromise that no seat actually holds.**

## Objects

### Motion
- **Title** — the move, in a phrase.
- **Sponsor** — the seat proposing it.
- **Description** — what specifically gets done.
- **Expected Benefit** — tied to the Success Definition.
- **Explicit Tradeoffs** — what this motion gives up. Named, not hand-waved.
- **Risks** — how it can go wrong.
- **Required Proof** — the evidence that would confirm it's working.
- **Stop Conditions** — what would make the board pull the plug after adopting it.

### Vote
- **Yes | No | Abstain**
- **Rationale** — why, from this seat's lens and governed consequence.
- **Disconfirming signal** — what would change this vote. A vote with no stated
  way to change it is dogma, not a position.

### Decision Record
- **decision** — what the board resolved.
- **rationale** — why this, over the refused alternatives.
- **refused** — what was *explicitly not chosen*, and why. The road not taken is
  part of the record; it's what a future convening checks against.
- **dissent[]** — the minority positions, preserved verbatim in each dissenter's
  voice. Never averaged away. A recorded dissent is how the board stays honest
  when the decision ages.
- **proof** — a single **30-day proof-of-life metric**: the measurable thing that,
  checked in 30 days, tells you the decision was right or wrong.
- **motions[]** — the motions considered.
- **votes{}** — the tally per motion, per seat.

## The minimum bar — unanimity means the board failed

Every board must surface **at least one genuine collision.** If every seat agrees,
the board has produced consensus theater (Forbidden #5) — the masks nodding along,
not the mirrors thinking. When you get unanimity:

1. **Re-run with sharper seat separation.** The seats are probably too alike, or
   one governor went decorative. Re-pick seats so the governed consequences
   genuinely pull against each other, and reassert each seat's distinct
   disagreement move.
2. **If still unanimous after sharpening, report the failure honestly** — do not
   fabricate a disagreement to look rigorous. Say: "This board reached genuine
   unanimity; either the decision is less contested than it looked, or these seats
   don't cover the real tension — here's my read on which." A manufactured
   collision is as broken as a suppressed one.

## Differential test within the board

Run `differential-test.md` *between the seats*, live: if two seats' Truth-Round
diagnoses, proposals, or cross-examination lines could **swap speakers** without
anyone noticing, the board is register-bled — at least one seat has collapsed into
the archetype instead of the person. Sharpen the seat (reload its Card, its Lens,
its Signature) or drop it. Attribution must be possible from the contribution
alone, no name attached.

## Quick mode vs deep mode

**Quick mode** (default for a fast read): one agent holds all seats' Cards and
their governed-consequence pushback in a single context, and runs the phases
internally. Faster, cheaper, good when the seats are well-specified and the
decision is medium-stakes. Risk: seats bleed toward each other in one context —
lean hard on the within-board differential test.

**Deep mode** (for high-stakes or contested decisions): dispatch each twin as its
own subagent for **round 1** (Truth Round + Proposals), so each seat reasons in an
isolated context and can't converge prematurely. Then run a **collision pass** —
one orchestrator stages Cross-Examination, Motions, and Votes across the returned
positions. Isolation in round 1 buys genuine independence; the collision pass buys
the confrontation. Use deep mode whenever premature convergence would be expensive.

## Doctrine Library — decisions accrete into memory

A board's output is not just the decision — it's a **precedent.** Adopted motions
and their Decision Records accumulate into a **Doctrine Library**: a searchable
lineage of what this set of twins has decided and why. Each convening should:

- Check the library for prior related decisions before convening (has this board,
  or these seats, ruled on something adjacent? what did they refuse last time?).
- On close, append the Decision Record and any principle the board adopted
  ("we don't ship without a 30-day proof metric") to the library.

Over time this becomes institutional memory across convenings — a decision lineage
you can trace, and a way to catch a board contradicting its own past doctrine
without noticing.

## Guards on every convening

- **Each seat stays a twin.** Peer not servant; no seat flatters the decision or
  softens to consensus. The contract binds every seat.
- **Preserve every shadow.** A seat's blind spot stays in — if the cost governor
  would miss a technical risk because cost is all it sees, keep that. Governed
  consequences are narrow on purpose.
- **Simulate, don't commit.** The Decision Record is a prediction and a
  provocation. Anything needing the real people's sign-off is flagged, never
  asserted as their actual ratification.
- **One sanctioned footer per seat's voiced output**, per `twin-contract.md` —
  the only break in character, where the simulator says where it's least sure this
  is how that person would actually vote.
