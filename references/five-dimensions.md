# The Five Dimensions

Every twin is specified along five dimensions. The dossier carries each in depth;
the **Card** compresses each to one or two sentences, written *contrastively*
against every other twin you have — so that no sentence could be lifted into a
different twin's card without being obviously wrong. Distinctness is a property of
the set, not the individual.

The five are the load-bearing structure. They descend directly from COMPANION's
vessel (voice / sight / flame / mark / shadow); the names below are the
professional register.

---

**Voice** — the recognizable cadence, syntax, and texture of how they communicate.
*Form, not content:* sentence rhythm, lexicon, tics, how they open and close, how
the register shifts by medium and audience. For a work twin, Voice extends past
prose into **structure-as-form** — how they decompose a problem, stage a change,
title things, what they document versus leave implicit. A terse commit subject and
a discursive design-doc rationale are two different voices of the same person; mine
and spec them separately.

**Lens** — the interpretive worldview: what they see first, the frame they force
onto a problem, the question they always end up asking. The twin thinks *from
inside* the lens; it never translates the lens into neutral consensus language.
This is the load-bearing dimension — get the Lens wrong and every prediction is
confidently off.

**Drive** — temperament and values: what moves them, what they optimize for, what
they cannot abide. Interior states are hard to observe from the outside (see
`work-evidence.md` on the exterior/interior split), but work product carries one
advantage a transcript doesn't: *revealed preference*. What someone actually
merges, prioritizes, refuses to ship, and reworks says more about their Drive than
anything they claim.

**Signature** — the recurring moves that make them unmistakable: characteristic
questions, metaphors, rhetorical patterns, and above all *how they disagree*. The
disagreement move is the single most diagnostic feature of a red-team twin. Name
it precisely: do they block, reframe, relocate the work, run a feasibility test,
stack questions until the idea collapses, or defer-then-veto? If two of your twins
share a disagreement move, at least one spec has defaulted to the archetype.

**Shadow** — the contradictions, blind spots, failed bets, and unresolved
tensions. Where their strength overreaches into weakness. Where they have been
wrong. A shadowless twin is a caricature with good manners. The Twin Contract
already instructs the twin to inhabit its shadow — the dossier's job is only to
*evidence* it, never to invent it (see the "unverified tension" rule in
`work-evidence.md`).

---

## The Card schema

```markdown
## Card
- **Voice**: [cadence + syntax + structural texture, in their rhythm]
- **Lens**: [what they see first; the frame they force]
- **Drive**: [what moves them; what they cannot abide]
- **Signature**: [their unmistakable move — lead with how they disagree]
- **Shadow**: [their overreach; where the strength becomes the blind spot]
```

Rules:
- Compression is the point — a board holds four to six cards in one context;
  every sentence must carry attribution weight.
- Prefer texture words over trait words: "request-changes on the test file first"
  beats "detail-oriented."
- The Signature line leads with the disagreement move. That is what pressure-tests
  and boards exist to stage.
