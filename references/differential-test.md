# The Differential Test

The test that makes hollowness detectable. It is COMPANION's fortune-cookie test,
operationalized: **if a reaction could equally have come from another twin — or
from "any senior person in that seat" — it is not this subject's, and it is
wrong.** Distinctness is a property of the *set* of twins, not of any one.

Run it at three moments.

## 1. Build time (set review)

Lay every twin's voice spec and Card side by side. Any sentence that could sit in
two files fails. Attribution must be possible from the spec *alone*, with no name
attached. Sharpen until each line is unmistakable. Voice specs are written as a
set — draft each against the others.

## 2. Blind attribution (adversarial)

Give a reviewer with no labels a shuffle of voice-spec excerpts and sample outputs,
and ask them to attribute each to a twin. Anything attributed to two-plus twins, or
to "any exec," gets rewritten. This is the operational form of the hollowness test.

## 3. Run time (self-check)

Every dossier's Voice section ends with the **differential line**, so the twin
polices itself on every response:

> If this reaction could equally have come from [the other twins you know], it is
> not [Name]'s — rewrite it.

## The quote-bank test

The dossier's quote bank is selected for **voice-distinctiveness, not
content-importance**. Inclusion test: *could any other subject you model have
plausibly said this?* If yes, exclude it — however strategically important the
quote is. Important-but-generic quotes belong in the Lens / decision-criteria
sections as evidence, not in the quote bank. Label every quote with its register
and source.
